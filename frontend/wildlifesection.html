Wildlife & Nature Trails

Discover the diverse flora and fauna of Maharashtra.

Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve

Located in Chandrapur, Tadoba is Maharashtra's oldest and largest national park. It is a premier tiger reserve in India, known for its significant population of the majestic Bengal tiger.

Know More

Sanjay Gandhi National Park

A unique national park located within Mumbai's city limits. SGNP is home to ancient caves, leopards, and diverse flora. It attracts over two million visitors annually for nature trails and wildlife spotting.

Know More

Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary

Famous for the Indian Giant Squirrel (Shekru), the state animal of Maharashtra. This sanctuary is a biodiversity hotspot in the Western Ghats, offering lush green forests and rare species.

Know More

Karnala Bird Sanctuary

Located near Panvel, this sanctuary is a paradise for bird watchers. It hosts resident and migratory birds and offers a scenic trek to the Karnala Fort situated within the sanctuary.
